Carved into the rugged landscape, the pass derives its name from the intricately crafted stone serpents that line the path, their piercing eyes seeming to watch over those who dare to traverse its treacherous route.

Legend has it that Serpent's Pass was once a sacred pilgrimage route, undertaken by ancient cultures seeking to commune with the spirits of the mountains. In an act of reverence and devotion, skilled artisans carved the stone serpents as both guardians and markers, imbuing them with a sense of mystical power. These serpents were believed to possess the ability to guide and protect travelers, warding off malevolent spirits and ensuring safe passage through the formidable terrain.

Over the centuries, Serpent's Pass became a renowned route for adventurers, explorers, and traders, drawn to its breathtaking views and the allure of its ancient history. However, the pass demanded respect and tested the mettle of even the most seasoned travelers.
