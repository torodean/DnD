Pine Resin

A sticky, amber sap collected from pine trees, used for its adhesive and healing properties. A small lump (enough for one balm) costs 3 sp and weighs 1 oz.

Recipe

  • Locate a mature pine tree and make a shallow cut in the bark with a sharp blade.
  • Collect the oozing resin in a small container over 1 hour.
  • Store in a sealed jar and keep cool to prevent it from hardening prematurely.
